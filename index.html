Concept 1 — What Is Medicare?

Federal · Age 65+ or Disability · NOT Income-Based

  • Administered by CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services)
  • Four Parts: A, B, C, D — each with different billing implications
  • NOT income-based — that is Medicaid

⚠️ Exam Trap — Admitted vs Observation

Physically in hospital ≠ always Part A

  • Formally admitted → Part A (inpatient)
  • Observation status → Part B (outpatient) — even if overnight
  • Patient responsibility is often HIGHER under observation
  • Always verify admission status before billing

Concept 2 — Medicaid

Joint Federal-State · Income-Based · Payer of Last Resort

  • Joint federal-state — each state runs its own version
  • Based on income — NOT age (that is Medicare)
  • Payer of last resort — always billed LAST
  • Eligibility changes month to month — verify every visit

Concept 3 — Medigap

Private · Supplements Original Medicare · NOT Part C

  • Private insurance sold by private companies
  • Supplements Original Medicare only — not MA (Part C)
  • Covers gaps like the 20% Part B coinsurance
  • Billing: Medicare → EOB → Medigap as secondary
  • Part C replaces. Medigap supplements. Different workflows.

Concept 4 — TRICARE

Department of Defense · Military Beneficiaries

  • Active duty military, retirees, and families
  • Administered by the Department of Defense
  • Types: Prime (HMO-like) · Select (PPO-like) · TRICARE for Life (retirees — Medicare primary)
  • Do not assume civilian plan rules apply

Concept 5 — COB Basics

When a patient has 2+ plans, bill in order: Primary → Secondary → Patient

  • Primary pays first at full benefit
  • Secondary receives primary EOB then pays its share
  • Total payment cannot exceed 100% of the charge
  • Medicaid is ALWAYS last — payer of last resort regardless of other coverage

⚠️ Critical COB Rules

These rules appear directly on the CBCS exam

  • Medicaid = always payer of last resort
  • Medicare + Medicaid = Medicare primary, Medicaid last
  • Auto accident = auto/no-fault insurance is primary for accident-related medical bills
  • Workers' comp injury = workers' comp is always primary

Concept 6 — Birthday Rule

For dependent children covered under BOTH parents' plans

  • The parent whose birthday falls EARLIEST in the calendar year has the primary plan
  • Uses month and day only — the birth YEAR does NOT matter
  • Example: Mom = March 15, Dad = July 4 → Mom's plan is primary
  • If same birthday: plan in effect longer is primary

Concept 7 — Premium

Monthly cost to keep the policy active

  • Paid to the insurer every month regardless of services used
  • NOT collected by the medical office at the visit
  • Does NOT count toward the deductible or OOP max
Concept 8 — Deductible

Annual amount patient pays BEFORE insurance begins paying

  • Resets every plan year
  • Critical billing check: Has it been met? How much remains?
  • If $400 of a $1,000 deductible is met → patient still owes $600 first
Example: $1,000 deductible — $600 already met → $400 remaining

Concept 9 — Copay vs Coinsurance

Fixed dollar vs percentage — very different things

  • Copay = FIXED dollar per visit (e.g., $30/visit). Collect at time of service.
  • Coinsurance = PERCENTAGE of allowed amount AFTER deductible (e.g., 20%). Varies by service cost.
  • Medicare Part B: patient pays 20% coinsurance after deductible (unless Medigap covers it)
Concept 10 — Out-of-Pocket Maximum

Annual ceiling — once met, insurance pays 100% of covered services

  • Once the OOP max is reached → patient owes $0 for covered services for rest of year
  • Premium does NOT count toward OOP max
  • Billing check: If OOP max is met → collect $0 copay/coinsurance

🔵 Medicare Parts

Part AHospital Insurance — inpatient, SNF, hospice. Bill A when ADMITTED.
Part BMedical/Outpatient — physician, lab, DME. Pays 80%; patient owes 20%.
Part CMedicare Advantage — REPLACES Original Medicare. Bill the private plan, NOT CMS.
Part DPrescription Drugs — pharmacy only. Bill the PDP. In-office drugs = usually Part B.
Part C ≠ MedigapPart C REPLACES. Medigap SUPPLEMENTS. Completely different billing workflows.
Observation ≠ Part AObservation status = outpatient = Part B even if overnight in hospital.
